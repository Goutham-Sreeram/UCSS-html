Photosynthesis is a vital biological process through which plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy. This process not only sustains the organisms that perform it but also supports life on Earth by producing oxygen and organic compounds.

Overview of Photosynthesis

Definition and Importance

Photosynthesis is defined as the process where phototrophic organisms, primarily green plants, use sunlight, carbon dioxide (CO2), and water (H2O) to produce glucose (C6H12O6) and oxygen (O2).

This process is crucial for several reasons:

  • It produces oxygen, which is essential for the respiration of most living organisms.
  • It converts solar energy into chemical energy stored in glucose, which serves as food for plants and other organisms that consume them
